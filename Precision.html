Precision Agriculture



Real-time soil monitoring



Real-time soil monitoring involves using sensors to continuously measure various soil parameters like moisture, temperature, pH, and nutrient levels, and transmitting this data wirelessly to a central system for analysis and visualization. This information helps farmers make data-driven decisions for optimized irrigation, fertilization, and crop management, ultimately leading to increased yields and resource efficiency



Key Components of Real-time Soil Monitoring:

  • Moisture sensors: Measure the amount of water in the soil.
  • Temperature sensors: Measure the soil temperature.
  • pH sensors: Measure the acidity or alkalinity of the soil.
  • Electrical conductivity (EC) sensors: Measure the salt content in the soil.
