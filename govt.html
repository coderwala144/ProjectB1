Soil Health Card Scheme

The Soil Health Card (SHC) Scheme is an initiative by the Government of India, launched in 2015, aimed at improving soil health and fertility. The scheme provides farmers with a soil health card that contains information on the nutrient status of their soil and recommendations for nutrient management to enhance productivity.

Key features of the scheme include:

  • Soil Testing: Regular soil testing is conducted to assess soil health indicators.
  • Recommendations: The card offers crop-wise recommendations for fertilizers and nutrients tailored to individual farms.
  • Frequency: Soil analysis is performed once every three years to monitor changes and guide farmers in taking remedial actions. This scheme is designed to promote sustainable agricultural practices and improve the overall productivity of farms across the country
