Wheat 𓇢𓇢

📝DESCRIPTION :

Wheat is a globally important cereal grain from the genus Triticum, with T. aestivum (common wheat) being the most cultivated. It originated around 9600 BC in the Fertile Crescent. Botanically, the wheat seed is a caryopsis (a type of fruit).Wheat covers more land than any other food crop—about 220.7 million hectares in 2021. Its global trade surpasses all other crops, and with a production of 771 million tonnes in 2021, it is the second most-produced cereal after maize. Since 1960, wheat production has tripled and is expected to continue growing, largely due to the food industry's demand for gluten.Nutritionally, wheat is a major source of carbohydrates and the top source of vegetable protein in human diets (around 13% protein). Whole grain wheat also provides essential nutrients and dietary fiber. However, gluten in wheat can cause health issues for some individuals, such as coeliac disease and other gluten-related disorders.


🌟Benifits of cotton:

  • 🌾 Nutritional Benefits
  • Rich in Carbohydrates – Provides energy for daily activities.
  • Good Source of Protein – Contains about 13% protein, making it a top source of plant-based protein.
  • High in Fiber (Whole Wheat) – Promotes digestion, prevents constipation, and supports gut health.
  • Packed with Nutrients – Contains B vitamins (like folate, niacin, thiamin), iron, magnesium, zinc, and selenium.
  • 🧠 Health Benefits
  • Heart Health – Whole wheat may reduce the risk of heart disease due to its fiber and antioxidant content.
  • Weight Management – High-fiber content can increase satiety, helping with weight control.
  • Regulates Blood Sugar – Whole wheat has a lower glycemic index than refined grains, aiding blood sugar control.
  • Supports Metabolism – B vitamins and magnesium help in energy production and metabolic functions.
  • 🌍 Global Importance
  • Widely Available & Affordable – Staple food in many countries, accessible to a large population.
  • Supports Food Security – Grown in large quantities globally, contributing to stable food supplies.

    • 👨‍🌾Cultivation Practices

  • Land Preparation – Plough and level the field.
  • Seed Selection – Use certified, high-yielding varieties.
  • Sowing – Sow in rows (Oct–Dec); use drill method.
  • Irrigation – Water at key stages (rooting, tillering, flowering, grain filling).
  • Fertilization – Apply NPK fertilizers based on soil test.
  • Weed & Pest Control – Use herbicides/insecticides as needed.
  • Harvesting – Harvest when grains turn golden and hard.
  • Storage – Dry and store grains in cool, dry places.
