Vertical farming



Vertical farming is a technique where crops are grown indoors on stacked layers, often using controlled environment agriculture and soilless farming methods like hydroponics. This approach maximizes space and optimizes plant growth by controlling factors like light, temperature, and water.


Components of Vertical farming:

  • Indoor growing: Crops are grown in controlled environments, typically indoors in buildings or repurposed spaces like shipping containers.
  • Stacked layers:Factors like temperature, humidity, light, and ventilation are precisely controlled to optimize plant growth.
  • Controlled environment agriculture:Methods like hydroponics, aquaponics, and aeroponics are used to grow plants without soil.
  • Artificial lighting::LED lights are often used to provide the necessary light for plant growth.
